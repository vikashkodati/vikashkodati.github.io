My Story

Over the past two years, my work with the IIT Guwahati Alumni Association (IITGAA) has been about building a foundation that lasts. We established the first-ever legal entity for IITGAA in the U.S., creating a structure that allows us to receive tax-deductible donations. This was more than just paperwork; it was about enabling a sustainable future where alumni contributions directly support our alma mater.

One of my priorities has been transparency and engagement. I started sharing quarterly reports to keep everyone informed, making sure the work we do is visible and accountable. These reports weren’t just about numbers; they were about trust and showing that every contribution, big or small, is being put to work efficiently.

I’ve also focused on fostering entrepreneurship within our alumni network. We partnered with TechSoup to offer discounts on technology and services, helping alumni leverage tools that might otherwise be out of reach. Although our application for a $50,000 AWS grant didn’t succeed, we secured recurring nonprofit grants, ensuring ongoing support for our initiatives.

Our efforts to organize webinars not only provided knowledge-sharing opportunities but also empowered alumni by giving them a platform to moderate these sessions. This isn’t just about learning; it’s about leadership and making sure our alumni are at the forefront.

In the financial realm, I spearheaded the creation of long-term endowment funds and built the tax-exempt infrastructure needed to handle donations and payments efficiently, whether at conferences or online. We now have physical credit card machines (Zettle) for onsite donations, making it easier for alumni to contribute.

At the PanIIT Conference, I represented IIT Guwahati, strengthening relationships with key PanIIT leaders. These connections are crucial for expanding our influence and opportunities across the broader IIT alumni network.

I also worked to align IITGAA with other IIT alumni associations, discussing the creation of a separate foundation to support our activities. This involved multiple meetings with alumni across different IITs, where we talked about governance, election processes, and the relationship between the Alumni Association and the proposed foundation.

Finally, my focus has always been on individual alumni. Whether it was helping someone find like-minded individuals for a startup, navigating contract issues, or simply making the right introductions, I’ve been there, building relationships that matter.
